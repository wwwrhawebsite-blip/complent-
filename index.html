⚖️ Know Your Rights

Legal Rights & Resources

Knowledge is power. Understand the laws that protect you.

Key Indian Laws

📋
Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005
Demand information from any public authority within 30 days.
You can file an RTI to ask any government body about public spending, decision-making, or official records. Fee: ₹10. If denied, appeal to First Appellate Authority, then Central Information Commission. RTI is your most powerful tool against corruption.
🛒
Consumer Protection Act, 2019
Rights against defective goods, unfair trade, and service deficiency.
File complaints at District Consumer Forum (claims up to ₹1 crore), State Commission (₹1-10 crore), or National Commission (above ₹10 crore). You can also file online at consumerhelpline.gov.in. No lawyer needed for district level!
👩
Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005
Legal protection, residence rights, and maintenance for abused women.
Contact a Protection Officer (PO) in your district. File a Domestic Incident Report (DIR). You can seek protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief. The process is free and POs must help within 3 days of receiving complaint.
🌿
Environment Protection Act, 1986
Report pollution, illegal dumping, and environmental damage.
File complaints with State Pollution Control Board. Under Section 19, citizens can file complaints in court against polluters. The NGT (National Green Tribunal) accepts environmental cases — no court fee for individuals. Email: complaints@cpcb.nic.in
👶
Right to Education Act, 2009
Free and compulsory education for children aged 6-14 years.
Every child has the right to free education in a neighborhood school. Schools cannot deny admission. 25% seats in private schools must be reserved for economically weaker sections. File complaints with State Education Department or NCPCR.
